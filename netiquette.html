What is Netiquette?

Netiquette (a portmanteau of "network etiquette") refers to the social code of the internet. Just like etiquette guides our behavior in real life, netiquette provides guidelines for polite and respectful online communication.

In today's digital world, where much of our communication happens through screens, understanding and practicing good netiquette is more important than ever. It helps create a more positive, productive, and respectful online environment for everyone.

Did You Know?

The term "netiquette" was first coined in the 1980s as internet usage began to grow. Many of the core principles established then remain relevant today, despite the massive changes in technology.

The 10 Core Rules of Netiquette

These fundamental principles will guide you in maintaining proper online etiquette across all digital platforms.

1

Remember the Human

The people you interact with online are real humans with feelings. Never say something online you wouldn't say in person, and remember that online interactions are often recorded.

2

Be Ethical

Illegal activities like pirating content are still breaking the law online. Consider the ethical implications of your digital actions, including using ad blockers.

3

Know Where You Are

Netiquette varies by platform. Observe community norms before participating ("lurk before you leap"). Professional forums require different behavior than social media.

4

Respect Time & Data

Be considerate of others' time when posting. Send messages only to relevant people and be mindful of file sizes and data limits when sharing content.

5

Make Yourself Look Good

Maintain a positive online presence. Avoid sharing embarrassing content, communicate clearly, and remember that online anonymity can be both a privilege and responsibility.

6

Share Expert Knowledge

Contribute valuable information to online communities. Share experiences (both positive and negative) and ensure the accuracy of what you post.

Why Netiquette Matters

Better Online Communities

Good netiquette fosters respectful interactions, reducing conflicts and creating more positive digital spaces for discussion and collaboration.

Professional Reputation

Your online behavior reflects on you professionally. Employers and colleagues often check digital footprints before making hiring decisions.

Personal Security

Following netiquette guidelines helps protect your privacy and security by encouraging thoughtful sharing of personal information.

The Digital Golden Rule

"Treat others online as you would like to be treated yourself. The anonymity of the internet is no excuse for poor behavior."

Sweet Rule #1: Remember the Human

When you are online, it is sometimes easy to forget the human. That is, the people you are interacting with online are real humans and not disembodied usernames or profile pictures. While it brings so many benefits, the internet can often dehumanise other people, especially when you are interacting with someone you do not know personally. For this, remember, it is important to remember the human. You should never say something to someone online that you would not say to him or her in person. It is also useful to remember that online interactions are nearly always recorded and backed up. If you act inappropriately, these recordings will serve as evidence against you.

All 10 Netiquette Rules

Rule 1: Remember the Human

The people you interact with online are real humans with feelings. Never say something online you wouldn't say in person, and remember that online interactions are often recorded.

Rule 2: Be Ethical

Illegal activities like pirating content are still breaking the law online. Consider the ethical implications of your digital actions, including using ad blockers.

Rule 3: Know Where You Are

Netiquette varies by platform. Observe community norms before participating ("lurk before you leap"). Professional forums require different behavior than social media.

Rule 4: Respect Time & Data

Be considerate of others' time when posting. Send messages only to relevant people and be mindful of file sizes and data limits when sharing content.

Rule 5: Make Yourself Look Good

Maintain a positive online presence. Avoid sharing embarrassing content, communicate clearly, and remember that online anonymity can be both a privilege and responsibility.

Rule 6: Share Expert Knowledge

Contribute valuable information to online communities. Share experiences (both positive and negative) and ensure the accuracy of what you post.

Rule 7: Keep Disagreement Healthy

Disagreements are natural, but keep them respectful. Focus on issues, not personal attacks, and provide constructive criticism when offering negative feedback.

Rule 8: Respect Other People's Privacy

Don't share others' private information without permission. This includes not using others' devices or accounts without consent.

Rule 9: Don't Abuse Your Power

If you have special privileges (like admin rights), use them responsibly and fairly, not to intimidate or unfairly advantage yourself.

Rule 10: Be Forgiving of Others' Mistakes

Everyone was new once. When someone makes a netiquette mistake, politely point it out privately rather than publicly shaming them.
